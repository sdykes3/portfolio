Alien Staff

The Alien Staff is an electronic staff intended to be carried by immigrants as a tool for facilitating communication. It includes a small monitor and speaker at head level, which broadcasts the story of the staff bearer’s journey. The base of the staff includes a transparent cylinder containing artifacts representing the life of the carrier, such as letters, visas, and objects from their home country.

This project was inspirational as an example of an intermediary communication artifact, as well as a visualization of complex personal information.

Wodiczko, Krzysztof. “Alien Staff.” Accessed Apr 14 2018. https://www.macba.cat/en/alien-staff-2515

Air Quality Balloons

Stacey Kuznetsov's air quality balloons are a great example of ecovisualization which provides a snapshot view in a specific location. The air quality balloons use sensors to update the color of each balloon’s LED in real time, indicating the detectable levels of pollutants in the air. Each balloon represents a different substance.

This project also employs spectacle computing, a strategy by which information is “vibrantly projected into the social sphere using expressive and tangible media" to draw attention. In choosing to put my own data on a garment as uncommon as the sash, I also leveraged some of this novelty factor to draw attention to my project.

Stacey Kuznetsov, George Noel Davis, Eric Paulos, Mark D. Gross, Jian Chiu Cheung. “Red Balloon, Green Balloon, Sensors in the Sky.” http://www.paulos.net/papers/2011/BalloonSensingUbicomp2011.pdf

Digital Ethereal

Long exposure photographyi is coupled with a device that registers WiFi signal and represents it as a color based on reception strength, visualizing WiFi signals as light.

This project inspired the theme of materiality in my data sash, using the materials of a project to show data that is always present but usually remains invisible, such as a person's carbon footprint.

We Were Strangers Once Too

An installation in New York City's Times Square. Various metal poles show national information on foreign-born occupants of New York. Viewed from the correct angle, the data displayed on the poles align to create a heart shape.

District 5

A collection of bent metal tubes representing charts of various crime rates on decline, placed outside a police station in Chicago. This was one of the first projects I referenced and it motivated my exploration of data placement, which eventually led me into the realm of wearables.
